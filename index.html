Collaborate. Build. Pitch.

4 Hours to Product is a competition where a bunch of creators from different backgrounds come together to brainstorm, prototype, test, and produce a basic version of some type of app. Basically, a 4-hour hackathon from idea to prototype.

How It Works

Meet at the AL.com building in downtown Huntsville, AL for CoWorking Night on Wed. Jan 4th, 2017 at 6:00PM. Contestants will then be split up into teams of 3 people. Next, the teams will collaborate to develop and pitch an app idea to a panel of judges. Teams will have then have until 9:00PM to work on their business plan, pitch presentation, and prototype. At 9:00pm, time is up and all teams must stop work and meet in a conference room to share a 5 minute presentation. Each team will also get a 5 minute question round lead by the panel of judges. After all teams have presented, the judges will declare one team a winner.

Rules

Be Respectful

  • Be kind to others and their ideas. Listen to other peoples ideas in full and do not be quick to shoot them down.

 

Competition

  • Teams should consist of 2 - 3 people.
  • Teams assignments will be randomly chosen by the event sponsor.
  • Each team must pick and base their product on one of the categories provided by the Event Sponsor.
  • Teams have 15 minutes to select a category, otherwise, one will be assigned for them.
  • Once a category has been selected by a team, no other team may select that category.
  • Teams have the ability to pivot to another category at any time, as long as, the category has not been selected by another team.
  • The product that's being created must have a main focus on the Team's selected category, however, it is totally fine if the product overlaps with another category even when selected by another team. Example: Both Real-Estate and IoT are categories. It's perfectly fine to build an IoT device for Real-Estate.
  • Teams are encouraged to recruit, collaborate, and ask for feedback with any CoWorkers who are not participating in the event.

Ownership
