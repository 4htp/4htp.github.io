Collaborate. Build. Pitch.

Category: Internet of Things (IoT)

 

4 Hours to Product is a competition where a bunch of creators from different backgrounds come together to brainstorm, prototype, test, and produce a basic version of some type of app. Basically, a 4-hour hackathon from idea to prototype.

Our next competition will be centered around the Internet of Things also know as IoT. Micheal Whitley, CTO of EngeniusMicro and an industry leader in IoT will present the state of the industry address.

About the speaker: Michael Whitley is a licensed professional engineer and founder of EngeniusMicro, a small business focused on developing uncommon sensors that see beyond the ordinary to improve safety, find failures before they happen and increase value. EngeniusMicro has 15 employees in the Huntsville and Atlanta areas and is located in the Times Building downtown, "above" Below the Radar. Michael holds 3 patents and has published over 30 peer-reviewed publications related to sensor technology.

How It Works

Meet at the Real Estate Row off Pratt Ave in Huntsville, AL for CoWorking Night on Wed. Feb 1st, 2017 around 6:00PM. Micheal Whitley, CTO of EngeniusMicro, will then present on the state of the IoT (Internet of Things) industry to get us started. Those wanting to participate will then be split up into teams of 3 people. Next, the teams will collaborate to develop and pitch an app idea to a panel of judges. Teams will then have until 9:00PM to work on their business plan, pitch presentation, and prototype. At 9:00pm, time is up and all teams must stop work and meet on stage to share a 5 minute presentation. Each team will also get a 5 minute question round lead by the panel of judges. After all teams have presented, the judges will declare one team a winner.

Rules

Be Respectful

  • Be kind to others and their ideas. Listen to other peoples ideas in full and do not be quick to shoot them down.

 

Competition

  • Teams should consist of 2 - 3 people.
  • Teams assignments will be randomly chosen by the event sponsor.
  • Each team must base their product on the category provided by the Event Sponsor.
  • Teams are encouraged to recruit, collaborate, and ask for feedback with any CoWorkers who are not participating in the event.
  • Work can be outsourced to remote workers, if possible.

 

Ownership

  • All teams will retain full ownership of the idea and creation. CoWorking night is here to help participtants realize their dreams.
