Collaborate. Build. Pitch.

4 Hours to Product is a competition where a bunch of creators from different backgrounds come together to brainstorm, prototype, test, and produce a basic version of some type of app. Basically, a 4-hour hackathon from idea to prototype.

How It Works

Meet at the AL.com building in downtown Huntsville, AL for CoWorking Night on Wed. Jan 4th, 2017 at 6:00PM. Contestants will then be split up into teams of 3 people. Next, the teams will collaborate to develop and pitch an app idea to a panel of judges. Teams will then have until 9:00PM to work on their business plan, pitch presentation, and prototype. At 9:00pm, time is up and all teams must stop work and meet in a conference room to share a 5 minute presentation. Each team will also get a 5 minute question round lead by the panel of judges. After all teams have presented, the judges will declare one team a winner.

Rules

Be Respectful

  • Be kind to others and their ideas. Listen to other peoples ideas in full and do not be quick to shoot them down.

 

Competition

  • Teams should consist of 2 - 3 people.
  • Teams assignments will be randomly chosen by the event sponsor.
  • Each team must pick and base their product on one of the categories provided by the Event Sponsor.
  • Teams have 15 minutes to select a category, otherwise, one will be assigned for them.
  • Once a category has been selected by a team, no other team may select that category.
  • Teams have the ability to pivot to another category at any time, as long as, the category has not been selected by another team.
  • The product that's being created must have a main focus on the Team's selected category, however, it is totally fine if the product overlaps with another category even when selected by another team. Example: Both Real-Estate and IoT are categories. It's perfectly fine to build an IoT device for Real-Estate.
  • Teams are encouraged to recruit, collaborate, and ask for feedback with any CoWorkers who are not participating in the event.
  • Work can be outsourced to remote workers, if thatâ€™s even possible.

 

Ownership

  • All teams will retain full ownership of the idea and creation. CoWorking night is here to help participtants realize their dreams.

Categories

  • Internet of Things
  • Mobile Security

  • Social Shopping

 

Details

Internet of Things - Create an app that can be used to interface and/or control an internet / bluetooth enabled device.

 

Mobile Security - Create an app focused on security with a mobile platform. The app can use the security features of a smartphone to create transactions between people and/or business safer. The app could also be used to help secure private data on a smartphone.

 

The Physical Web - Create an app to work with The Physical Web project by google. The Physical Web is an open approach to enable quick and seamless interactions with physical objects and locations. More Info: https://google.github.io/physical-web/

 

Real Estate - Create an app with a focus on the Real Estate industry. The app can aim to make any part of the Real-Estate purchasing process better like Mortgages, Appraisals, Inspections, Agents, Brokers, Marketers, etc. The app can also deal the the Real-Estate market as a whole for investment such as Flippers, Banks, etc. The app should not, however, be for a product specific to the a home such as a smart lightbulb, smart appliance, etc. Other than that, anything related to Real Estate goes.

 

Sharing Economy / On-Demand Service Economy - Create an app for the Sharing Economy like Airbnb or the On-Demand Service Economy like Uber. Do not create an app to compete with Airbnb or Uber directly.

 

Social Shopping - Create an app with a focus on providing a shopping experience from social networking site like Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, linked-in, etc.
